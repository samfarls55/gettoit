# GetToIt v1.1 — Quiz Redesign & Verdict Engine PRD

The canonical design write-up is [[../50_product/v1.1-quiz-amendments]]. This PRD is the implementation specification derived from it. Where the two ever diverge, the design doc wins and this PRD should be corrected.

## Problem Statement

A group member answers the GetToIt quiz, but the verdict often lands on a place that does not reflect what they actually wanted. Three concrete failures, from the member's perspective:

- **The quiz asks the wrong things.** Walk-minutes and a single generic vibe slider do not capture what makes a member happy with a restaurant — what they are craving, what they can spend, the kind of place they want to discover.
- **The engine averages the group.** A polarizing pick can win the aggregate even though one member quietly hates it — the member follows along and carries resentment, the exact post-decision defection the product exists to prevent.
- **The last and most important question is fake.** Q5 ("Regret?") renders placeholder sample restaurants instead of real candidate venues (bug-03 / issue #43). The Foursquare API is never called with the member's real answers, so the verdict is keyed on hardcoded data.

The result is a verdict the group nominally agreed to but does not feel ownership of — which defeats GetToIt's north star of *followed-through* verdicts.

## Solution

Redesign the quiz around a three-bucket input model and five decision-shaping questions, fetch real candidate venues per member after their answers are captured, and replace the verdict engine with one that protects the worst-off member.

- **Three buckets.** *Profile* (rarely changes — allergies, dietary restrictions, cuisine NEVERS, stored on the account). *Parameters* (consistent across a session — geography, meal time, group size, service shape, transport mode, set pre-quiz by the initiator). *Questions* (vary per member — the 5-question quiz).
- **Five questions.** Q1 cuisine craving (multi-select, capped at 3), Q2 spend cap (hard ceiling), Q3 reputation/discovery, Q4 vibe energy (Quiet -> Chill -> Social -> Lively -> Rowdy), Q5 preference probe.
- **Q5 preference probe.** Each member rates three *real* candidate venues 1-5 on excitement. The three cards are a strict factorial — each deviates from the member's stated profile on exactly one axis (cuisine, reputation, vibe) — so the ratings reveal how much each axis truly weighs.
- **Per-member fetch.** A member's completion of Q1-Q4 triggers a Foursquare fetch tailored to their answers; the group candidate pool is the running union of all members' fetches.
- **New verdict engine.** EBA prune (drop venues failing any hard veto) -> per-member preference scoring -> satisficing floor (keep venues every member scores at or above threshold T) -> maximin tiebreak (pick the highest minimum member score) -> empty-floor cascade.

## User Stories

**Profile**
1. As a group member, I want to set my allergies once on my account, so that no session ever surfaces a venue that could harm me.
2. As a group member, I want to set my dietary restrictions (vegan, halal, kosher, gluten-free, etc.) once, so that every session respects them without re-asking.
3. As a group member, I want to record cuisines I will never eat, so that they are permanently excluded from my recommendations.
4. As a group member, I want my profile to persist across sessions, so that I configure it only once.

**Parameters (pre-quiz)**
5. As a session initiator, I want to set where the group is meeting before the quiz, so that all candidates are near the group.
6. As a session initiator, I want to set the meal time, so that only venues open then are considered.
7. As a session initiator, I want to set the group size / social context, so that the recommendation fits the occasion.
8. As a session initiator, I want to choose the service shape (dine-in indoor/outdoor vs takeout pickup/delivery), so that candidates match how the group wants to eat.
9. As a session initiator, I want to set the transport mode (walk/drive), so that the search radius matches how far the group will travel.
10. As a joiner, I want the session parameters applied to me automatically, so that I do not re-enter shared settings.

**The five questions**
11. As a group member, I want to pick up to 3 cuisines I am craving, so that the recommendation leans toward what I actually want tonight.
12. As a group member, I want a "No preference" option on cuisine, so that I can honestly signal flexibility instead of faking a craving.
13. As a group member, I want to be prevented from selecting more than 3 cuisines, so that I am nudged to be decisive.
14. As a group member, I want to set a spend cap, so that the verdict never exceeds what I can afford.
15. As a group member, I want my spend cap treated as a hard ceiling, so that an expensive venue is never forced on me.
16. As a group member, I want to pick a reputation/discovery preference (Popular / Hidden gem / Classic / New / No preference), so that the standing of the place matches what I want.
17. As a group member, I want to set the energy I want (Quiet -> Chill -> Social -> Lively -> Rowdy), so that the verdict matches the night's mood.
18. As a group member, I want Q5 to show me three real nearby candidate venues, so that my final and most important answer is grounded in reality, not placeholders.
19. As a group member, I want to rate each Q5 card 1-5 on excitement, so that the engine learns how much each preference axis matters to me.
20. As a group member, I want each Q5 card to deviate from my stated profile on exactly one axis, so that my ratings reveal my true preference weighting rather than just confirming a perfect match.
21. As a group member, I want to advance through the quiz without it stalling, so that the quick ritual holds.
22. As a group member, I want Q5 to never show a placeholder or sample restaurant, so that the verdict is keyed on real venues (closes bug-03).

**Fetch and pool**
23. As a group member, I want candidate venues fetched only after I complete Q1-Q4, so that the pool reflects my actual answers.
24. As the system, I want each member's fetch to be N+1 parallel Foursquare calls — one per craved cuisine plus one general call — so that every craved cuisine is well-stocked and the pool still carries non-craved variety.
25. As the system, I want the group candidate pool to be the running union of all members' fetches, so that breadth is preserved for the verdict.
26. As the system, I want each member's preference function to re-score new venues as the union grows, so that no member's scores go stale.

**Verdict**
27. As a group member, I want the verdict to consider only venues that pass every member's hard vetoes, so that nobody is handed something they cannot use.
28. As a group member, I want the verdict to pick a venue every member rates at or above the acceptability threshold, so that the winner is something the whole group is genuinely OK with.
29. As the worst-positioned member, I want the verdict to maximize the minimum member score, so that I am protected rather than averaged over.
30. As a group member, I want the verdict to relax soft preferences (then widen radius) before giving up when no venue clears the floor, so that the session still produces an answer.
31. As a group member, I want the engine to re-weight my stated preferences from my Q5 ratings, so that the verdict reflects which axis I truly care about most.
32. As a group member, I want a stated preference overridden only when my Q5 ratings consistently contradict it, so that a single odd rating does not flip my answer.

**Verdict firing**
33. As a session initiator, I want a "close voting" control, so that I can produce the verdict without waiting on a straggler.
34. As a group member, I want no shot clock on the quiz, so that I am never rushed.
35. As a session initiator, I want the verdict to fire automatically once all participants finish, so that I need not act when everyone is done.
36. As a session initiator, I want a verdict even if I am the only respondent, so that a dead session still resolves.

## Implementation Decisions

The full rationale for every decision below is in [[../50_product/v1.1-quiz-amendments]]. Modules are labelled A-K for cross-reference with the Testing section.

**Schema**
- **(H) `votes` table migration.** Replace the typed-per-question columns (`q1_vetoes`, `q2_budget`, `q3_walk_minutes`, `q4_vibe`, `q5_regret`) with generic Q1..Q5 jsonb slots, so quiz content can change without a migration. Per-session question variability is carried in jsonb.
- **(I) Profile storage.** New per-account storage for allergies, dietary restrictions, and cuisine NEVERS.

**Pure modules (deterministic, no I/O)**
- **(A) `buildPreferenceFunction`.** Input: a member's Q1-Q4 answers plus their three Q5 ratings. Output: `prefFn(venue) -> score 1-5`. Internals: stated-weight initialization (axes seed at equal 1/3; an explicit "No preference" zeroes that axis); soft re-weight (partial blend toward Q5-revealed weights, blend constant `alpha`, cohort-zero default 0.5); hard-contradiction override (strict two-condition trigger; action is demote-to-no-preference, never invert); score normalization (each axis 1-5, weighted average, soft non-match scores ~2 — below threshold T so the satisficing floor has teeth). `match=5`, `soft-non-match~2`, `T=3`, `alpha=0.5` are cohort-zero defaults, tunable post-cohort.
- **(B) `decide()` — verdict engine rewrite.** Full rewrite of the existing `verdict-engine.ts` (currently an EBA-with-relax-cascade consuming old field names by hand). New flow: EBA prune (drop venues failing any member's hard vetoes — profile dietary/allergies/NEVERS, parameter geo/meal-time, Q2 spend cap) -> per-member scoring via cached `prefFn`s -> satisficing floor (keep venues every member scores at or above T) -> maximin tiebreak (highest minimum member score) -> final tiebreak (highest sum, then random) -> empty-floor cascade (relax T, then widen radius, then terminal no-spot screen). Runs server-side so the verdict is identical for every member. The engine consumes inputs through a schema-driven mapping layer, not by hardcoded field names.
- **(C) Q5 factorial card generator.** Input: a member's Q1-Q4 answers and their fetched pool. Output: three strict-factorial cards, each deviating on exactly one axis (cuisine, reputation, vibe) and matching the other two; never a 100% match. Which two of a member's cuisines are probed is chosen by a member-local rule (best card-generation feasibility in the pool) — group state never influences card selection.
- **(D) Foursquare fetch planner.** Input: a member's Q1 cuisines, Q2 price, plus shared parameters and profile. Output: N+1 Foursquare call specs (N = craved cuisines, 1-3) — one category-filtered call per craved cuisine plus one mandatory general call. Pure planning; no I/O.

**I/O and stateful modules**
- **(E) Axis scorers.** Per-axis venue match functions feeding `prefFn`: cuisine (set membership — clean), reputation (derived from Foursquare venue metadata), vibe (graded distance on the 5-point energy scale). Reputation and vibe scoring depend on the Foursquare data-sourcing research (see Out of Scope) — their interface (`venue -> 1-5`) is fixed, their internal metadata mapping is not yet specified.
- **(F) Foursquare fetch executor.** Executes the N+1 call specs in parallel, unions and dedupes results by venue id. Wraps (D). This replaces the bug-03 failure mode: Foursquare was called once, early, with empty `PlacesFilters()` before any quiz answer existed.
- **(G) Running-union pool manager.** Server-side. Holds the growing group pool, triggers `prefFn` re-scoring of new venues for already-completed members, caches per-member scores.

**iOS surfaces**
- **(J) `QuizCoordinator` rework.** New Q1-Q5 semantics: Q1 cuisine multi-select with the cap-3 and mutually-exclusive "No preference" toggle logic; Q2 spend cap; Q3 reputation chip picker; Q4 vibe energy scale (Quiet..Rowdy); Q5 factorial preference probe over real candidates.
- **(K) Pre-quiz setup surface.** New iOS screen for the initiator to set session parameters (geography, meal time, group size, service shape, transport mode). Consult `design-system/` before building — this is a new surface and will need design-system review.

**Architecture decisions (cross-cutting)**
- The candidate pool is a **union**, never an intersection — the engine is built to take a broad set and narrow it (EBA, then satisficing). Intersecting fetch sets would hard-cut soft signal and front-run the narrowing with no empty-pool safety net. The "Venn middle" is produced by the satisficing floor, which intersects on scores after EBA intersects on hard vetoes.
- The verdict fires when **all participants complete Q5** or when the **initiator closes voting** — no timer, no shot clock. Minimum is one member (the initiator alone) in the edge case where nobody else responds.
- Reputation is a **client-side-scored axis**, not a fetch filter — the query-chip technique is retired (the factorial requires reputation variety in every pool, so reputation cannot strict-filter the fetch).
- Cuisine cannot strict-filter the fetch either (same factorial reason); the general call in every per-member fetch supplies the non-craved variety.

## Testing Decisions

A good test asserts **external behavior through the module's public interface**, never implementation details — feed defined inputs, assert the observable output. Tests are written for modules **A, B, C, D, E, F**. G-K are built but not given dedicated tests (G is verified indirectly through B's tests and manual session runs; H by running the migration; I/J/K by manual and TestFlight verification).

- **(A) `buildPreferenceFunction`** — pure unit tests. Feed canned Q1-Q4 answers and Q5 ratings, assert the returned `prefFn` produces the expected 1-5 scores for canned venues. Cover: equal-weight init, "No preference" zeroing, soft re-weight blend, the override trigger firing and not firing, score normalization and the below-T soft non-match.
- **(B) `decide()`** — pure unit tests. Feed a canned pool and canned per-member `prefFn`s; assert the winner. Cover: EBA prune, the satisficing floor, maximin selecting the worst-off-protecting venue over a higher-sum polarizing pick, the empty-floor cascade.
- **(C) Q5 factorial card generator** — pure unit tests. Feed canned answers and a canned pool; assert each card deviates on exactly one axis, no card is a 100% match, and the member-local cuisine selection is honored.
- **(D) Foursquare fetch planner** — pure unit tests. Feed canned answers; assert the N+1 call specs (count, per-cuisine category filters, the mandatory general call, hard filters applied).
- **(E) Axis scorers** — unit tests against the fixed `venue -> 1-5` interface. The cuisine scorer is testable now; reputation and vibe scorer tests follow once the data-sourcing research fixes their metadata mapping.
- **(F) Foursquare fetch executor — boundary assertion.** A recording proxy client asserts the N+1 calls actually fire, with the correct count, for the happy path, and that the planner's specs are forwarded intact. This is the explicit regression guard for bug-03's silent no-call failure mode.

Prior art: `ios/Tests/Q5CandidatesLoaderTests.swift` (canned places output through a loader) and `ios/Tests/QuizSessionAssemblerTests.swift` (the existing recording `PlacesProxyClient` boundary assertion) are the patterns to follow for the iOS-side tests. `verdict-engine.ts` tests are TypeScript unit tests.

## Out of Scope

- **Q5 card info-availability UX** — default vs expanded card content, in-flow vs modal restaurant details. Deferred to a fast-follow after the backend logic ships.
- **Foursquare API filter-surface research** — a prerequisite research task, not part of this build. It unblocks the reputation and vibe axis scorers (E) and the graded-axis question below.
- **Reputation and vibe metadata mapping** — the internal logic of the (E) scorers is blocked on the research above. Their interfaces are in scope; their internals are not yet specified.
- **Graded axis in the factorial** — vibe is cardinal but the factorial treats axes as binary keep/drop; what "drop the vibe axis" means precisely is an open design item, downstream of the data-sourcing research.
- **Card-generation pool-starvation fallback** — behavior when a thin per-member pool cannot furnish three valid factorial cards is undefined; an edge-case fallback to be decided at implementation time.
- **Visual redesign** — the design system (Sunset Pop) is locked; the pre-quiz surface (K) consumes existing tokens and components.
