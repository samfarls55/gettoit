# v1.1 Quiz Amendments

Documents how the v1.1 quiz redesign amends [[v1-design-locks]]. The v1 locks remain valid as v1 history; v1.1 amendments layer on top with stated reasons (Path B, chosen during the 2026-05-15 grilling session).

Origin: bug-03 (Q5 placeholder, Foursquare fires once with empty filters before Q1-Q4 are captured). The founder pivoted from fixing the wiring to redesigning the questions first. This document is the redesign output. Status is `settled` — the design grill closed 2026-05-15; the remaining §8 items are research and implementation tasks, not open design questions. Implementation is specified in [[../10_prds/v1.1-quiz-redesign-prd]].

## 1. The three-bucket model

User input splits across three buckets, by this discriminator rule:

- **Profile** — never or rarely changes per user. Set once, stored on the account.
- **Parameters** — consistent across all participants in a single session. Set pre-quiz by the initiator.
- **Questions** — vary person-to-person. The 5-question quiz.

### Profile
- Allergies
- Dietary restrictions (vegan, halal, kosher, gluten-free, etc.)
- Cuisine NEVERS (negative-only — "I will never eat sushi")

### Parameters
- Geography (where the group is meeting)
- Meal time (drives Foursquare `open_at`)
- Group size / social context (solo / duo / group)
- Service shape: dine-in (indoor/outdoor) vs takeout (pickup/delivery)
- Transport mode (walk / drive — drives the `radius` default)

## 2. The five questions

| # | Question | Signal | Wire |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 | Cuisine craving | soft preference | `fsq_category_ids` |
| Q2 | Spend cap | hard NEED veto | `min_price` / `max_price` |
| Q3 | Reputation / discovery | soft preference | client-side scoring |
| Q4 | Vibe energy | cardinal | client-side scoring |
| Q5 | Preference probe | cardinal probe | client-side scoring |

- **Q1 Cuisine craving** — multi-select cuisine chips, capped at 3 picks (or "No preference"). Positive direction; complements the profile-level cuisine NEVERS. Soft signal, not a hard cut. See §3 for the cap rationale and the Q1-input-cap vs Q5-probe-cap distinction.
- **Q2 Spend cap** — $ / $$ / $$$ / $$$$ picker, interpreted as a ceiling. Hard NEED veto per Lock 2; aggregated as the intersection (lowest cap across members).
- **Q3 Reputation / discovery** — chip picker: Popular / Hidden gem / Classic / New / No preference. Client-side-scored soft axis, derived from Foursquare venue metadata (rating count, rating value, venue age) — not a fetch filter. See §5 (reputation scoring) and §8 (data-sourcing open item).
- **Q4 Vibe energy** — cardinal 5-point scale: **Quiet → Chill → Social → Lively → Rowdy**. One axis only: energy / loudness, not formality. Foursquare cannot filter this; client-side scoring only.
- **Q5 Preference probe** — see §3.

## 3. Q5 — the preference probe

Q5 is a preference *probe*, not a tiebreaker or a selector. Each member sees three real candidate venues and rates each 1-5 on excitement ("How excited does this make you?"). The ratings reveal the member's preference weighting; the engine extrapolates from the probe to score the full candidate pool.

### Card generation
- Each member sees their **own** three cards (rolling — no shared or locked set).
- Cards are drawn from a pool that respects that member's hard vetoes (allergies, dietary, NEVERS, spend cap) but are **not** tailored to their soft preferences.
- **Never a 100% match** — every card deviates from the member's stated Q1-Q4 profile on at least one axis. A perfect-match card teaches nothing and fails to force tradeoff consideration.
- **Strict factorial** — with three axes (cuisine, reputation, vibe) and three cards, each card drops exactly one distinct axis and matches the other two. Each axis is therefore kept by two cards and dropped by one; its weight is read by comparing the drop-card score to the two keep-card scores.
- Cards are info-rich: photo, name, one-line cuisine/vibe tags, and a tappable expand for details (menu, reviews, more photos) without leaving the quiz flow.

### Stated-vs-revealed extrapolation (framework (ii))
- Q1-Q4 are **stated** preferences (priors). Q5 is **revealed** preference on concrete venues.
- **Soft case** — the probe reveals the weight hierarchy among stated axes (a member who states "casual Mexican" with implied equal weight may reveal casual >> Mexican). The engine **re-weights**; it does not override.
- **Hard-contradiction case** — a stated axis is **overridden** when the revealed preference floors it consistently across multiple cards that deviate on different other axes. Consistency across differing deviations proves the contradiction is real, not a confound.
- **Asymmetric confidence** — a consistent negative (multiple cards sharing a feature, all floored) is high-confidence; act decisively. A lone positive (a single elevated card) is low-confidence; mild lean only, do not over-fit.

### buildPreferenceFunction internals

`buildPreferenceFunction(Q1-Q4 answers, three Q5 ratings) -> (venue) -> score 1-5`. It scores any venue as a weighted blend of three soft axis-match scores: cuisine (Q1), reputation (Q3), vibe (Q4). Q2 spend is a hard veto pruned in EBA, not a soft axis here.

**Stated-weight initialization** — before Q5 ratings adjust anything, the three axis weights seed at equal (1/3 each). Q1-Q4 give the member's *position* on each axis, not how much they care; revealing the weight hierarchy is Q5's job, so the prior is deliberately neutral. Exception: an explicit "No preference" answer (the Q3 chip, or Q1 left empty) is a genuine zero-weight signal — that axis is zeroed and its weight redistributed equally to the surviving axes. Edge case: if a member no-prefs two of three axes, only one axis carries weight and the Q5 factorial degenerates (each card still drops a distinct axis, but two of the three drops are weightless). Acceptable — flagged, not blocked.

**Soft re-weight** — Q5 reveals the weight hierarchy. For each axis, `marginal_value = avg(two keep-card ratings) - (drop-card rating)`, floored at 0 and normalized across axes to give `w_revealed`. Final weights are a *partial* blend toward the revealed signal, not a full replacement: `w_final = (1 - alpha) * w_prior + alpha * w_revealed`, where `w_prior` is the no-preference-adjusted equal prior from initialization. Blending toward a non-zero prior is deliberate — an axis the member positively selected in Q1-Q4 is never discounted all the way to zero by a thin 3-card probe; only an explicit "No preference" zeroes an axis. `alpha` is a global constant tunable post-cohort like T; cohort-zero default 0.5. Degenerate cases: all three cards rated equal means every `marginal_value` is 0, so `w_final` stays at the prior. A drop-card rated *above* both keep-cards yields a negative `marginal_value` floored to 0 soft contribution; genuine reversals of that kind are handled by the hard-contradiction override below.

**Hard-contradiction override** — when Q5 reveals a stated axis is not merely low-weight but actively wrong, it is overridden rather than re-weighted. The trigger is strict and two-condition, chosen because each axis has only two keep-cards (a thin evidence base): override fires for an axis only when *both* keep-cards score strictly below the drop-card, AND the drop-card is rated 4 or 5. "Both below" — not the averaged margin — rules out a confound from the keep-cards' differing other-axis deviations; the genuinely-excited drop-card is positive proof that the stated position, not card mediocrity, is the problem. Action: **demote to no-preference** — the stated position stops scoring points and the axis collapses to the no-preference state (weight zeroed, redistributed to the surviving axes). Override does *not* invert toward the drop-card's value: that card also kept its other two axes, so its high score is already explained by them — "the member likes the drop-card's value" is confounded, not proven. A demoted axis is neutral, never negative: a cuisine the member positively picked never becomes a liability.

**Score normalization** — `prefFn(venue)` returns a 1-5 score on the same scale as the satisficing threshold T (~3). Each axis (cuisine, reputation, vibe) produces its own 1-5 match score; the venue score is the weighted average across axes with nonzero weight (demoted / no-preference axes drop out, remaining weights renormalize to sum to 1). A weighted average of 1-5 values is itself 1-5, so T stays interpretable on every axis. A match scores 5; a **soft non-match scores ~2 — below T, not at it**. This is deliberate: it gives the satisficing floor (§4 step 3) real teeth, so soft preferences can eliminate a venue in aggregate, not merely rank it. Over-pruning is the expected, handled case — the empty-floor cascade relaxes T when soft preferences prune too hard; were non-match pegged at T, both the floor and the cascade would be inert for soft preferences. Fairness is protected by the weighting: a non-match on a low-weight axis barely dents the score, so a venue only sinks below T when the member genuinely cares about the missed axes. Vibe is the one graded axis — scored by distance on the 5-point energy scale, 5 at an exact match descending toward the bottom at the opposite end. Open tuning item: a vibe mismatch may warrant scoring lower than a cuisine/reputation non-match (a Quiet member in a Rowdy venue is actively unhappy, not merely indifferent). `match=5`, `soft-non-match~2`, and `T=3` are all cohort-zero defaults, tunable post-cohort.

### Q1 multi-select and the cuisine cap

Q1 cuisine is multi-select, capped at **3** picks (UI: "pick up to 3", plus a mutually-exclusive "No preference"). The cuisine-axis match is set membership — a venue whose cuisine is in the member's set scores as a match. Two caps are distinct and must not be conflated:

- **Q1 input cap = 3.** A cap exists to force selectivity (anti-paralysis) and bound the override inference; 3 is the UX sweet spot. Beyond 3, the honest signal is "No preference."
- **Q5 probe cap = 2.** Strict factorial supplies only two cuisine keep-cards, so Q5 probes at most 2 of a member's cuisines. This is sufficient: Q5 measures the cuisine *axis* (weight + contradiction), which is axis-level, not per-cuisine. A 3rd picked cuisine needs no probe — cuisine is a soft scoring axis, so the 3rd cuisine rides along as a free scoring-positive and never needs validating.

Which 2 cuisines Q5 probes is chosen by a **member-local** rule: the 2 with the best card-generation feasibility in the candidate pool (this also covers rare-cuisine pool starvation). Group state never influences Q5 card selection. Q5 is a per-member *measurement*, and its stimulus must not be curated by other members' votes — doing so would make the quiz vote-order-dependent and contaminate the preference reading. The group "Venn middle" narrowing is delivered entirely by the verdict engine (satisficing floor + maximin, §4); the 3rd cuisine contributes to it simply by being in the member's scoring set. Measurement (the quiz) and aggregation (the verdict engine) stay separated.

Override on a multi-pick is whole-axis: Q5 cannot isolate one cuisine within a set (only two keep-cards), so a fired override demotes the entire selected cuisine set to no-preference. For a 3-cuisine pick, the untested cuisine is demoted on inference (the testable cuisines underperformed, so the stated set is treated as unreliable); demote-to-no-preference keeps this harmless — the untested cuisine becomes neutral, not penalized.

## 4. Verdict aggregation — satisficing floor + maximin tiebreak

The locked engine is EBA (prune) + Satisficing (accept) from [[framework-comparison]]. v1.1 realizes it as:

1. **EBA prune** — remove venues failing any member's hard vetoes (profile dietary/allergies/NEVERS, parameters geo/meal-time, Q2 cap). Produces the group pool.
2. **Per-member scoring** — each member's preference function (Q1-Q5) scores every pool venue, normalized 1-5.
3. **Satisficing floor** — keep venues where every member scores at or above threshold T.
4. **Maximin tiebreak** — among floor survivors, pick the highest minimum member-score. Protects the worst-off member; serves the follow-through north-star.
5. **Final tiebreak** — highest sum, then random.
6. **Empty-floor cascade** — relax T progressively (silent soft-pref relaxation per [[v1-scope]] no-survivor rule), then widen radius, then a terminal no-spot screen.

T is a global engine constant, tunable post-cohort. A Q5 card rated 3 is the natural acceptability bar.

Rejected: pure sum / utilitarian aggregation (polarizing-pick defection risk — the Kim 2023 backfire mechanism). Rejected: geometric centroid / facility-location 1-median (utilitarian, and assumes a shared uniformly-weighted metric space that per-member weighting, categorical axes, and hard-veto cliffs all violate).

## 5. The real-time decision function

- The candidate pool is built from **per-member fetches**, not one global fetch. Each member's completion of Q1-Q4 triggers a Foursquare fetch tailored to that member's quiz answers and profile, from which their three Q5 cards are generated. The fetch is the only slow / expensive step; there is one fetch per member (typically 2-6 members).
- **Each per-member fetch is N+1 parallel Foursquare calls** (N = the member's craved cuisines, 1-3). One call per craved cuisine — `categories` filtered to that cuisine, plus the hard filters (geo / radius / open_at, Q2 price) — so each craved cuisine is guaranteed well-stocked rather than skewed by a single capped multi-category call. Plus **one general call** — category-agnostic, same hard filters — which is mandatory: it supplies the factorial's cuisine-drop card and the broad non-craved variety, and without it a multi-cuisine member's pool would be all-craved with no cuisine-drop card. The member's fetch result is the union of the N+1 calls, deduped by venue id. Calls run in parallel, so wall-clock latency stays ~one call. Only cuisine varies the call criteria — it is the only one of the three Q5 soft axes Foursquare can filter on; reputation and vibe are not filterable, hence client-side scored.
- The **group pool is the running union** of every member's fetch — union, not intersection. The verdict engine is built to take a broad candidate set and narrow it (EBA prune, then satisficing floor); intersecting fetch sets would hard-cut soft signal, front-run that narrowing, and leave no empty-pool safety net. The "Venn middle" is produced by the satisficing floor (§4 step 3) — which intersects on member *scores* after EBA has intersected on hard vetoes — not by intersecting pools.
- Per-member scoring runs **incrementally** the moment a member completes Q5; results are cached. As the union grows (a later member's fetch adds venues), earlier members' `prefFn`s re-score the new venues — cheap, since scoring is sub-millisecond and `prefFn` is a stored pure function. Only the fetch cost scales with group size.
- The verdict fires when **all participants have completed Q5**, or when the **initiator manually closes voting** — a "close voting" control, with no timer or shot clock, so a true straggler is never rushed (the initiator holds the power, not a clock). `decide()` then runs the prune / floor / tiebreak over the cached scores of whoever has completed Q5 at fire time. Compute is sub-millisecond. In the rare edge case where no one but the initiator responds, the verdict runs on a single member.
- Runs **server-side** (`verdict-engine.ts` Supabase edge function) so the verdict is identical for every member.

### Reputation scoring — query-chip technique retired

An earlier design sent the Q3 chip as a preset keyword in the Foursquare `query` parameter (the "query-chip technique"). It is retired. The Q5 factorial requires every member's pool to contain reputation variety — a non-matching-reputation venue for the reputation-drop card — so reputation cannot strict-filter the fetch, the same constraint that keeps cuisine from filtering. Reputation is therefore a **client-side-scored axis**, derived from Foursquare venue metadata (rating count for Popular, high-rating-low-count for Hidden gem, venue age for Classic / New). The exact metadata-to-score mapping is unresolved — see §8.

## 6. Amendments to v1-design-locks, lock by lock

- **Lock 1 (quiz length 5)** — survives. Five questions; re-evaluate post-cohort.
- **Lock 2 (signal stack)** — partially amended. The vibe cardinal axis (Q4) survives. The "three hard vetoes at Q1-Q3" structure no longer holds: the original hard-veto axes (dietary, distance, cuisine veto) have moved to profile and parameters, so the in-quiz vetoes are fewer. Q5's role shifts from "tiebreaker scalar over survivors" to "preference probe."
- **Lock 3 (tiebreaker = regret-of-omission scalar)** — fully overridden. Q5 is a preference probe, not a tiebreaker over survivors. The cardinal-intensity nature survives (1-5 ratings); the mechanism does not. The verdict's tiebreaker is now the maximin rule in §4.
- **Lock 4 (verdict-screen copy framework)** — untouched.

Other amendments:
- Cuisine moved from profile (per [[questions-profile-vs-session-split]]) to the session quiz (Q1). Reason: food cravings are emotional and right-now; a profile-locked cuisine like loses to a session craving every time. Cuisine NEVERS stay profile-level.
- Excitement framing — Q5 copy is "How excited does this make you?" rather than a regret prompt. Mathematically equivalent to regret-of-omission; positive framing.

## 7. Iteration history (for posterity)

The Q5 design iterated several times during the grill before settling:
1. Lock 3 original (rate the survivors) — rejected for signal-stability under partial quorum.
2. Archetype rater (rate three vibe-x-adventurousness cards) — rejected on a user-shoes critique (too abstract, high cognitive load, redundant with Q3/Q4, not decision-shaping).
3. Real-candidate rater with candidate-set-lock-at-2-members — rejected because locking after two members bakes in early preferences and makes later members' ratings meaningless.
4. **Preference probe with diverse per-member cards** — settled.

## 8. Open items

Design (open grill items):
- Client-side data sourcing for the reputation and vibe axes — reputation derives from Foursquare venue metadata (rating count / value / age), but New and Classic depend on a reliable venue open-date Foursquare may not expose; vibe (energy / loudness) has no Foursquare field at all. Where each venue's reputation and vibe values come from is unresolved.
- Graded axis in a crisp factorial — vibe is cardinal (a point on Quiet..Rowdy), but the factorial treats every axis as binary keep/drop. What "drop the vibe axis" means for a graded axis is underspecified.
- Card-generation feasibility / pool starvation — the strict factorial assumes each per-member pool can furnish three valid cards; the fallback when it cannot (rare cuisine, thin pool) is undefined.

Settled this session: `buildPreferenceFunction` (see §3); the fetch architecture — per-member N+1 blended calls and the running union (see §5); reputation moved to client-side scoring; verdict fire conditions (see §5).

Implementation:
- Conditional-quiz schema — `votes` table jsonb for per-session question variability.
- `verdict-engine.ts` rewrite — currently consumes `q1_vetoes` / `q2_budget` by name; needs a schema-driven mapping layer.
- Pre-quiz setup surface — new iOS surface for the session parameters.

Deferred:
- Q5 card info-availability UX — default vs expanded content, in-flow vs modal. Fast-follow after the backend logic ships.
- Foursquare API filter-surface research filed to `60_engineering/research/`.
