# Product

## Register

product

## Users

GetToIt is for friend groups, couples, and small coworker crews deciding where to eat on a phone, often at the end of the day with little patience for back-and-forth. The primary mobile user is an Account member acting on a Plan, inviting others, answering a short quiz, waiting for quorum, and acting on a single verdict.

## Product Purpose

GetToIt ends food decision paralysis by turning a group's structured inputs into one follow-through-ready venue verdict. Success means the group trusts the procedure, commits to the place, and actually goes.

## Brand Personality

Decisive, social, alive. The product should feel like a warm friend closing the loop, not a judge, host, or black-box algorithm.

## Anti-references

Avoid ranked lists, top-three reveals, vote tallies, win-counts, "your turn" logic, celebration framing, slot-machine rerolls, and coercive labels like "Confirm" or "Accept." Avoid generic restaurant cards, corporate dashboards, and decorative motion that does not express group state or verdict finality.

## Design Principles

One answer, fast: the verdict must be unambiguous in the first few seconds.

Proof before persuasion: rule and voice receipts must show that the outcome came from group inputs.

Voluntary commitment: "I'm in" should feel like joining the table, not submitting to the app.

Alive, not chaotic: social state and motion should make the group feel present without reopening debate.

Correctability has friction: reroll exists, but it should never feel like a free slot machine.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Default to high-contrast dark mode, 44pt minimum tap targets, readable text over gradients, and reduced-motion fallbacks. Motion should communicate state changes such as verdict arrival, ratification progress, dock closure, and reroll eligibility.
